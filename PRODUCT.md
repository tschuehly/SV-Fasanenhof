# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The site serves prospective members, current members, parents, visiting teams, course participants, local residents, sponsors, and volunteers of 1. SV Fasanenhof 1965 e.V. Most visitors arrive with practical questions: where training happens, who to contact, how to join, when courses take place, what the club offers, and whether the atmosphere feels approachable.

The primary context is local and time-sensitive. People often check the site before visiting the Logauweg grounds, choosing a department, registering for a Schnupperkurs, confirming training details, or finding club governance information. Content must be easy to scan on mobile, clear for non-technical users, and credible enough for parents, guests, and public stakeholders.

## Product Purpose

The website is the shared public home for 1. SV Fasanenhof 1965 e.V., bringing Verein, Fußball, Tischtennis, Bogenschießen, Standort, membership, and contact information into one cohesive static site. It replaces fragmented legacy sites with a single department-first structure.

Success means visitors can quickly understand the club, choose the right department path, trust that the information is current, and find a direct next action: visit training, join, contact a responsible person, attend a Schnupperkurs, or navigate to the Vereinsgelände.

## Brand Personality

Local, grounded, reliable.

The brand should feel like a long-standing Stadtteilverein with real people, real sport, and a visible place in Stuttgart-Fasanenhof. It should be warm without becoming sentimental, organized without becoming bureaucratic, and ambitious enough to respect the Bogensportabteilung's depth and competitive activity.

The emotional goal is quiet confidence: visitors should feel that the club is accessible, stable, volunteer-led, and worth showing up for.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic sports-club templates that feel empty, loud, or interchangeable. Avoid glossy startup marketing, neon performance-sports aesthetics, dark betting-site energy, overproduced hero-metric layouts, and stock-photo friendliness that hides the actual club.

Avoid invented filler copy. Fußball and Tischtennis should remain slimmer until real source material exists. Production copy must not mention migration, placeholders, curation notes, or internal editorial process.

Avoid visual systems that make the club feel like an app dashboard, an agency landing page, or a premium lifestyle brand. The site needs public-service clarity and local credibility.

## Design Principles

1. Make the real place visible. Use actual club, department, training, and venue imagery whenever possible so the site feels anchored at Logauweg rather than abstract.
2. Put practical paths first. Every major page should help visitors find training, contact, membership, location, or department-specific next steps without hunting.
3. Let departments have honest depth. Bogenschießen can be broad and content-rich; Fußball and Tischtennis should stay concise until their real content grows.
4. Sound like a responsible Verein. Copy should be direct, specific, and German-first, with real umlauts and no internal process commentary.
5. Balance warmth with structure. The interface should feel welcoming, but navigation, headings, tables, and cards must stay orderly enough for repeated use.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG 2.1 AA as the practical baseline. The audience includes children, parents, older members, first-time visitors, people using mobile phones on the way to the Gelände, and users who may not know club vocabulary.

Use strong text contrast, meaningful link labels, responsive layouts, clear focus states, descriptive image alt text, and readable German. Avoid motion that is required for comprehension. Keep contact routes and public access rules explicit, especially where safety and venue restrictions matter.
